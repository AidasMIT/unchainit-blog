Your website was probably built 3-5 years ago. Maybe on WordPress. Maybe on Wix or Squarespace. It works, mostly. But it's slow, hard to update, costs money every month, and your Google ranking hasn't moved in years.

Here's the thing: the cost of building a modern, fast, AI-optimized website has dropped by 80% since 2023. What used to take a developer 4 weeks now takes 4 days. And the result is better.

Here are 10 reasons to make the switch.

## 1. Your Site Is Slower Than You Think

Google's Core Web Vitals directly affect your search ranking. The average WordPress site scores 40-60 on PageSpeed Insights. The average Wix site scores even lower.

Modern AI-built sites score **95-100** because they:
- Generate optimized, minimal code (no bloat)
- Use modern image formats (WebP, AVIF) automatically
- Don't load 15 JavaScript files from plugins you forgot about
- Deploy on global CDNs (your site loads fast in Tokyo and Toronto)

**Speed = money.** Amazon found that every 100ms of latency costs 1% in revenue. For small businesses, slow sites mean lost customers who never wait for your page to load.

## 2. You're Paying Monthly for Things That Should Be Free

Wix: $17-45/month. Squarespace: $16-49/month. WordPress hosting + plugins: $30-80/month.

That's $200-960/year for... hosting a website.

Modern static sites host on Vercel or Cloudflare Pages for **$0/month**. Free SSL, free CDN, free deployments. You pay for your domain ($12/year) and nothing else.

**Annual savings: $200-950.**

## 3. AI Can Build Your New Site in Days, Not Weeks

In 2024, rebuilding a 5-page business website meant:
- Finding a developer or agency
- 3-6 weeks of work
- $2,000-5,000 budget

In 2026:
- AI tools (Lovable, Bolt, v0) generate working sites from descriptions
- A developer with AI assistance rebuilds your site in 3-5 days
- Cost: $500-1,500

The quality is equal or better because AI generates clean, optimized code without the technical debt that accumulates in WordPress over years.

## 4. Google Now Understands (And Rewards) Modern Sites

Google's ranking algorithm in 2026 heavily weights:
- **Page speed** (Core Web Vitals)
- **Mobile experience** (responsive design)
- **Structured data** (Schema.org markup)
- **Content quality** (E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

Modern frameworks handle the first three automatically. Your WordPress site needs 5 plugins to achieve what Next.js or Astro does by default.

## 5. AI Search Engines Need Structured Data

ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews — these AI search engines are becoming how people find businesses. They don't read your WordPress site the same way Google's crawler does.

AI search engines prefer:
- Clean HTML structure (not div soup from page builders)
- Schema.org JSON-LD markup
- Fast-loading, accessible pages
- Clear, well-organized content

Modern sites are built for both human visitors and AI crawlers. WordPress sites are built for... WordPress.

## 6. No More Plugin Updates, Security Patches, or "White Screen of Death"

Every WordPress owner knows the anxiety of clicking "Update All" and hoping nothing breaks.

Modern static sites have **zero server-side code**. There's nothing to hack, nothing to update, nothing to break. Your site is a collection of HTML files served from a CDN. It's as secure and stable as it gets.

## 7. Your Competitors Are Already Moving

Check your top 3 competitors' websites on [PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev). If any of them score above 90, they've probably already moved off WordPress.

The businesses that move first get:
- Better Google rankings (speed advantage)
- Lower bounce rates (faster load = more visitors stay)
- Better conversion rates (modern design + fast UX)
- Lower costs (no monthly platform fees)

## 8. Content Editing Without the CMS Headache

"But I need to edit my own content!" — valid concern, bad reason to stay on WordPress.

Modern options:
- **Headless CMS** (Sanity, Strapi) — clean editing UI, modern frontend
- **Markdown files** — edit text files, site rebuilds automatically
- **Notion as CMS** — edit in Notion, site syncs
- **Ask your AI assistant** — describe the change, AI implements it

All of these are simpler than navigating WordPress's admin panel with 47 menu items.

## 9. Mobile-First Is No Longer Optional

62% of web traffic is mobile. Your Wix template from 2022 is "responsive" — meaning it technically works on mobile but looks like a desktop site squeezed into a phone screen.

Modern sites are designed mobile-first:
- Touch-optimized interactions
- Properly sized text and buttons
- Images that load at the right resolution for each device
- Layouts that actually work on small screens, not just shrink

## 10. The ROI Math Is Obvious

Let's compare keeping your current WordPress/Wix site vs. rebuilding:

**Keep WordPress (annual cost):**
- Hosting: $200
- Plugins/subscriptions: $300
- Developer maintenance (4h/year minimum): $400
- **Total: ~$900/year**
- Plus: slow site losing you customers you'll never measure

**Rebuild with modern stack:**
- One-time rebuild cost: $1,000-1,500
- Annual hosting: $12 (domain only)
- Maintenance: $0
- **Year 1 total: ~$1,200**
- **Year 2+ total: $12/year**

The rebuild pays for itself in 14-18 months. After that, you're saving $800+/year and running a faster, more secure, better-ranking website.

2. **Get a quote** for a modern rebuild — it's cheaper than you think
3. **Plan the migration** — redirect old URLs, preserve SEO equity
4. **Launch and monitor** — watch your speed scores, rankings, and conversion rates improve
